**3. Tsinghua University Teams Up with Shengshu Technology to Open Source TurboDiffusion, Accelerating Video Generation by 200x**
Remember how generating a 5-second, 720p video with a Diffusion model used to take a whopping 4767 seconds (over an hour)? Well, guess what? A three-pronged approach: hybrid attention acceleration, efficient step distillation, and W8A8 quantization. And here's the kicker ‚Äì **almost no loss in image quality**! This means even regular consumer-grade GPUs can now dabble in real-time video generation. AI video is officially moving from "offline waiting" to "real-time preview" era. Mind blown! ü§Ø

**5. ChatGPT to Launch "Skills" Feature in Early 2026**

This means you'll soon be able to assemble AI capabilities like LEGO bricks, instead of training from scratch every time. Expect it to drop in January 2026.

**6. Liquid AI Releases 2.6B Parameter Model, Outperforming Billion-Parameter Competitors**
Small models are winning again! Liquid AI's LFM2-2.6B-Exp, with just 2.6 billion parameters, has **outperformed billion-parameter models** in multiple benchmarks, especially in instruction following. What's even more crucial? It was trained purely with reinforcement learning ‚Äì no supervised fine-tuning, no large model distillation. It's fully open-source and supports edge device deployment. So, in the future, running a small model on your phone might just be even better than a massive cloud-based one.

**7. Kuaikan Manhua Teams Up with MiniMax, AI Interactive Comics Are Here**
Comic characters can finally "come alive"! Kuaikan Manhua and MiniMax have teamed up to launch AI companion interactive comics. They're using **AI tech to enable multi-modal interaction and emotional connection with comic characters**. You can chat and interact with your favorite characters, not just passively read their stories. This is being hailed as the "fourth track of AI experience" in the comic industry. For anime/manga fans, this might just be one of the most exciting product forms to drop this year! ü§©

**8. Huawei Noah's Ark Lab Releases SCOPE, Enabling Agents to Evolve from Errors**
What's the biggest headache with AI Agents? They **see error messages but keep making the same mistakes** next time. Huawei Noah's Ark Lab, in collaboration with CUHK, just dropped the SCOPE framework to fix this! It enables an Agent's Prompt to self-evolve, learning from execution trajectories. In tests on the HLE benchmark, task success rates shot up from 14.23% to 38.64% ‚Äì more than doubling! This is a major breakthrough for building truly reliable AI Agents.

---

**9. Xiaohongshu Open Sources InstanceAssemble, Breaking Through Accuracy in Complex Multi-Instance Image Generation**
One of the biggest headaches in e-commerce? **Getting AI to precisely place multiple products in a single image**. Xiaohongshu's open-sourced InstanceAssemble framework tackles this head-on. It significantly boosts spatial alignment accuracy in complex scenarios using cascaded modeling and an Assemble-Attention mechanism. The best part? It only adds 0.84% to parameters and is compatible with mainstream base models. For anyone doing e-commerce design or poster generation, this is definitely a tool to keep an eye on!

**10. OpenAI Releases Holiday Custom Codex Model: GPT-5.2-Codex-XMas**
